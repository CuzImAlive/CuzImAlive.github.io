Research

My work focuses on non-opioid pain management through selective neural inhibition. I use photobiomodulation (near-infrared light) to transiently block nociceptive C-fibers, and develop electrophysiology pipelines to verify and characterize these effects.

Publications & Manuscripts

tSNIP: Transient Selective Neural Inhibition via Photobiomodulation

Manuscript in Preparation
2025–26

First-author manuscript characterizing the use of near-infrared photobiomodulation to selectively inhibit nociceptive C-fibers in rat sciatic nerve. Includes ECAP recording methodology and stimulus artifact subtraction pipeline.

Ongoing Work

ECAP Recording from Rat Sciatic Nerve

In Progress
2024–

Developed a compound action potential recording system using Plexon OmniPlex with needle electrodes, biphasic stimulation via A-M Systems Model 2100, and a MATLAB-based epoch extraction and artifact subtraction pipeline. Successfully recorded C-fiber ECAPs confirmed via nerve transection control.
